// WORK

// Klangkeller

During my main project, I collaborated with the Museum of Musical
Instruments in Berlin on the development of KlangKeller (German 
for “Sound Cellar”) — a new interactive sound playground for children
and young people. The goal was to transform the experience of musical
instruments from passive observation into active exploration and play.

Within this project, I was part of the Visual Branding and Navigation group.
My focus was on defining how visitors could be guided to the KlangKeller
and how the space’s visual identity could both fit the existing museum
architecture and introduce a fresh, contemporary atmosphere that would
spark curiosity among younger audiences.

My concept centered on the idea of circles — inspired by architectural 
elements and musical notation, particularly note heads. These circular 
shapes became the key element of the visual system, forming a kind of 
trail that leads visitors through the museum toward the KlangKeller. 
I worked on developing this wayfinding concept, designing signage that 
integrates new information seamlessly into the museum, and creating 
proposals for how the visual language could extend into the space itself.

We had the opportunity to build and test parts of the design within the 
museum, which allowed me to evaluate how the navigation elements 
functioned in the real environment and to refine the visual identity 
accordingly.


// Tacker

As part of a university module, the short film "TACKER" emerged from 
a collaborative group project—a cinematic exploration of a claustrophobic,
dystopian future. The project focuses on the visual staging of a world
where societal structures and individual freedom are pushed to their limits.
Within the large project group, I was primarily responsible for the visual
world of "TACKER"; my focus was on location and set design.


// Trash of Berlin Match

Trash of Berlin MATCH was created in direct collaboration with an artist
who photographs discarded objects lying on the street.

These unique images of urban "trash" form the complete design foundation.
Each playing card is an aesthetic fragment from the artist's series, 
challenging players to find the matching pair in the details of decay. 
And I turned the photos into a memory game, for which I was responsible
for the game's design.

// Figma: Timi App

The everyday assistance app Timi was specifically developed for the Apple Watch
and plans to seamlessly organize the daily routine through the integration of 
calendar and map data, automatically reminds users of tasks and appointments,
and accompanies them with unobtrusive situational prompts for an efficient and
stress-free daily life.


// Photo Series: Playground

There are nearly 2000 playgrounds in Berlin. Places that shape city life without
receiving special attention. I spy with my little eye something you don't 
see—this is the guiding idea behind the photo series Platzspiel, which directs 
the gaze toward these everyday urban spaces. During the day, they are full of 
life, laughter, and movement, but in the early morning hours, when the city is 
still asleep, they transform. The playground as part of the urban fabric, which
adults mostly only perceive in passing—as a space between memory, function, and
forgetting.


// Interaction Design: Washing Hands --MISSING MATERIAL

This interaction design uses playful elements and digital design to introduce

thereby promoting adherence to important hygiene measures.


// Tacker Shortfilm

This short film was developed in close collaboration with the film team, with a focus on set design and on-site organization.
The visual atmosphere was shaped through the arrangement of space, objects and materials, supporting the tone and narrative of the film. Working behind the scenes,
the role involved coordinating practical elements on set and helping to bring the overall vision into a cohesive environment.
The project highlights an interest in spatial design within a cinematic context, where visual details and structure contribute to the storytelling.
